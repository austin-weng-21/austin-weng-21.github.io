Identity, Values and Strengths

Core Values

Integrity, humor, kindness, fairness, commitment, responsibility, consideration for others, courage, independence, pragmatism, reliability, respect, freedom.

There was one customer approached us to fix his bike that has been quoted for $1200 of repairing cost and he is happy to pay us $1000 if we could fix it. We took a careful check and found the issue is not that serious. The total bill for this repair job was only $90 ( the bike never comes back for this issue). I was reported by the workshop manager about this case and as a director I could easily instruct my workshop manager to take $800 since we have $1000 agreed budget. This is easy money but I will never allow this thing to happen as this is totally against my values. The customer couldn’t believe it when we told him the job only costs him $90 instead of $1000 and he was so touched and said thank you to almost every person at my company. I always believe that it’s our responsibilty to build a trustworthy society and keep it for our descendants.

I was born in China and the collectivism culture has deeply influenced me. I was educated to be responsible for my families, helpful for my friends and considerate for others.

In terms of my strengths in learning, I’m a targeted-oriented, motivated person. Once I believe the knowledge is what I need I will be fully committed to it. I will devote myself to learn as much as I can spontaneously. My limitation might be biased learning. I tend to learn what I’m interested and what I believe is pragmatic. This limits my breadth of my knowledge.

When I was at university there was a study group that we had to team up to work on an assignment. I was quite opinionated then and insisted that my idea was the best idea. Without doubt, my other study mates were not happy about the fact that I wasn’t listening to their opinions. I tried very hard to persuade them, sometimes, maybe with loud voice. This was not effective at all. I learned from this case and realised that no matter I agree or not agree with someone’s idea I should always respect them by listening to their opinions. If I still believe that I have the best idea I will give them time and gradually persuade them with supporting evidences.
